# Study — expanded two-panel dataset across the GOE

Extends the Tumbiana–Gunflint pilot (`../pilot/`) into a test of whether the preservation signal
across the GOE is driven by redox or by silicification.

## Contents
- `expanded_dataset.md` — full two-panel dataset (6 chert biotas + cross-GOE shales) with sources.
- `data.csv` — tidy CSV of the chert panel (ordinal scores; for future quantitative work).
- `synthesis.md` — preliminary analysis + the headline findings + revised study design.
- `figures/panel_heatmap.png` — ordinal heatmap: morphological fidelity vs silicification vs GOE.

## Headline findings (see `synthesis.md`)
1. **Morphological fidelity tracks silicification, not the GOE.** Pre-GOE Strelley Pool is
   morphology-rich where silica-encapsulated; pre-GOE Tumbiana/Moodies are poor where not; post-GOE
   Gunflint/Sokoman/Belcher are silica-rich and rich. The GOE is not the determinant.
2. **The "redox shifted cleanly across the GOE" premise is contested** at the bulk-shale level
   (a diagenesis-aware reassessment finds no significant A–P Fe-speciation trend).

## Implication
The original manuscript's sulfate→pyritisation mechanism is rejected on three grounds; the revised
mechanism centres on early silicification (+ Fe-biomineralization), with the silica cycle as the
testable GOE-adjacent variable. This becomes the empirical core for the manuscript revision.
