# Pilot report — Tumbiana vs Gunflint: feasibility and a mechanism-revision finding

**Date:** 2026-07-12. **Purpose:** address peer-review concern #2 (no feasibility data) by
compiling real published Raman / Fe / preservation data for one matched pre-/post-GOE pair, and
give a preliminary, honestly-caveated read on the framework's predictions. Dataset in
`compiled_dataset.md`.

## 1. Feasibility finding (directly answers reviewer #2)

**Can a maturity-/lithofacies-matched Tumbiana–Gunflint pair be assembled?**
**Qualitatively yes; quantitatively, two gaps remain.**

- Both units are shallow-water, silicified, **low-grade** (Tumbiana "weakly metamorphosed", <300 °C,
  Decreane2021; Gunflint prehnite–pumpellyite facies, Floran&Papike1975; Alleon2016) — so a
  **coarse maturity match is defensible** (both sub-greenschist, ~150–300 °C), and lithofacies
  overlap is reasonable (stromatolitic chert/carb).
- Rich published Raman + nanoscale-Fe + preservation data exist for **both** (Lepot2009;
  Decreane2021; Lepot2017; Alleon2016; Wacey2013). The comparison is not hypothetical — the
  literature is substantial.
- **Gap A (matchability):** no published RSCM peak-T (R₂/Tₘₐₓ) for Tumbiana — only a mineralogical
  <300 °C bound. A *strict* quantitative maturity match cannot yet be claimed from published data;
  it needs new RSCM on Tumbiana (cheap, ~1 lab session).
- **Gap B (redox proxy):** no bulk Poulton–Canfield FeHR/FeT or Fe_py/FeHR for either unit — the
  available Fe work is nanoscale mineralogy/isotopes. Bulk Fe-speciation comparison needs new
  measurements, or a redesign using paired contemporaneous shales (Fe-speciation is a shale proxy;
  these hosts are cherts/carb).

**Bottom line for feasibility:** the pilot supports proceeding, but de-risks two specific
measurements (RSCM-T on Tumbiana; bulk Fe-speciation) and flags that bulk Fe-speciation may
require a shale-bearing companion rather than the cherts themselves.

## 2. Preliminary signal

**Prediction 1 (pre-GOE OM-rich but morphology-poor): directionally SUPPORTED.**
Tumbiana is rich in organic globules (an OM pool *larger* than its rare microfossils, Lepot2009)
but has only "a few putative filamentous microfossils." Gunflint carries diverse, diagnostic
morphospecies (Lepot2017). The asymmetry the framework predicts — abundant kerogen without
corresponding morphology before the GOE — is real in this pair.

## 3. Mechanism-revision finding (the important result)

The framework's **headline causal mechanism — GOE-driven sulfate rise → pyritisation enabled →
morphology preserved — is NOT supported by this pair, and the data point the other way:**

1. **Pyritisation occurred in BOTH formations, not only post-GOE.** Tumbiana (pre-GOE) has
   micropyrites, active microbial Fe respiration, and BSR-driven sulfurization of OM (Decreane2021;
   Lepot2009). Pyritisation is not a post-GOE novelty.
2. **Gunflint was locally sulfate-limited** (Wacey2013, via Lepot2017) despite the post-GOE oceanic
   sulfate rise — yet it has the *best* morphological preservation. High sulfate is evidently not
   the controlling variable for Gunflint's fidelity.
3. **In Gunflint, pyritisation is DESTRUCTIVE, not preservative:** "pyritization nearly obliterates
   organic structures" (Lepot2017, citing Wacey2013). The morphologies that *are* preserved carry
   intra-cellular **greenalite + siderite + Fe-sulfide**, interpreted as *in-vivo Fe
   biomineralization* (likely cyanobacteria), and are protected by **early silicification**
   (Alleon2016).
4. So the preservative pathways in the best-preserved post-GOE biota are **silicification +
   Fe-biomineralization**, with pyritisation a secondary, often destructive, process.

**Implication for the framework paper:** the specific "sulfate→pyritisation" mechanism should be
revised. A defensible revised mechanism is that post-GOE morphological richness reflects the
*interplay* of (i) early silicification (pervasive in both eras but facies-dependent),
(ii) Fe-mineralization (in-vivo and diagenetic), and (iii) the *balance* between BSR-driven OM
remineralisation (sulfate-linked, which can both sulfurize-protect OM and destroy it) and
mineral templating — rather than a single sulfate switch. Sulfate's role is real but subtler
than "enables pyritisation": it governs the OM-decay vs OM-sulfurization trade-off (Type-A vs
Type-B pools in Tumbiana, Lepot2009), which affects *what* OM survives more than whether
morphology is templated.

## 4. What this pilot changes about the study design

- **Re-prioritise the mechanism.** Silicification and Fe-biomineralization move from "also-ran"
  pathways to the leading candidates; pyritisation is reframed as a decay/destruction pathway to
  be *controlled for*, not the signal. The process-graph (Fig. 1 of the manuscript) needs a
  revised edge set: silicification and Fe-mineralization → morphology (positive); BSR/pyritisation
  → OM remineralisation (can be negative for morphology).
- **Add the two de-risked measurements** (RSCM-T on Tumbiana; bulk Fe-speciation, possibly via a
  paired shale) to the analytical programme; both are now shown to be necessary, not optional.
- **Use this pair as the pilot figure** in a revised submission — it demonstrates the asymmetry
  (P1) is real *and* that the mechanism is non-trivial, which strengthens (not weakens) the case
  for the full comparative quantitative study. A reviewer can no longer say "the comparison is
  untested"; they can say "the pilot shows the simple mechanism is wrong — here is the
  redesigned test."
- **Integrates with peer-review point #4 (unmasking vs evolution):** the OM-pool richness of
  Tumbiana (globules without diagnostic morphology) is consistent with latent diversity that is
  *morphologically invisible* pre-GOE — directly motivating the molecular-clock/biomarker
  discriminator the reviewer asked for.

## 5. Honesty caveats

- This is a literature compilation (n = 2 formations, ~6 primary studies read in detail), **not**
  new measurement. No Raman spectra or Fe-speciation numbers were measured here; where published
  numbers were not found, the gap is reported, not filled.
- "Sulfate-limited" Gunflint (Wacey2013) is a local porewater/environmental interpretation, not a
  global statement — the framework's basin-scale sulfate argument is not thereby falsified, only
  shown to be insufficient on its own.
- The revised mechanism is a hypothesis generated by the pilot, to be tested in the full study —
  not a conclusion.
